## The Founder Thesis

These are the criteria the entire scoring system was built around. They represent what Harel is optimizing for in a business. They should inform every analysis, filter, and research conversation.

### Must-Have Economics
- **Massive customer pool** — room to scale even with strong incumbents; not a niche with a ceiling
- **High margins** — gross margin potential that protects pricing power; not commodity economics
- **No commodity dependence** — not a business where the main offer is reselling a standard product
- **Distribution efficiency** — possible to acquire many customers quickly

### Operating Model Preferences
- **No employees, or a small team of skilled professionals** — no low-skill labor-heavy operations
- **Low starting capital** — should be possible to launch credibly on ~$40K or less
- **Fast path to first revenue** — doesn't require years of investment before cash comes in
- **Owner-independence potential** — the business model should eventually be structured so it doesn't depend on Harel being hands-on every day

### What's Acceptable That Others Might Avoid
- **Boring or crowded categories** are fine — if the economics are strong and there's room to compete, market excitement doesn't matter
- **Regulated categories** — not automatically disqualifying; regulation can be a moat if the compliance burden is manageable

### What's Not Acceptable
- Labor-heavy operations requiring many low-skill workers
- Pure commodity products with no differentiation path
- Businesses that require constant founder involvement forever by design

## Geography: What Jerusalem Means

Harel operates from Jerusalem, Israel. This shapes what's realistic:

- **Remote/global delivery is a major plus** — a business where customers can be anywhere unlocks far more market than a locally-constrained one
- **Local market size is limited** — Israel is a small market; any business that can only serve local customers faces a ceiling
- **Customer accessibility matters** — for some business types, Harel needs to be able to reach target buyers. "Can he get 10 conversations in 2 weeks?" is a real filter
- **Regulatory environment** — Israeli business formation, tax, and licensing rules apply; some categories may have different barriers locally

## The Scoring Framework (Reference)

When analyzing or discussing any business type, these are the 12 dimensions that matter. Scores are 0-3 (Weak / Emerging / Solid / Strong).

**Floor metrics** — must all score ≥ 2 (Solid) to be considered:
- `market_headroom` — large, expandable demand even with incumbents
- `margin_quality` — strong gross margin, protected pricing
- `distribution_efficiency` — customer acquisition without heavy paid spend

**High-priority metrics** (Tier 2, in shortlist average):
- `startup_capital_intensity` — low upfront cost to launch credibly
- `speed_to_first_revenue` — quick path to first paying customer
- `team_model_fit` — works with no or few skilled employees
- `recurring_revenue_potential` — retainers or repeat business vs. one-off sales
- `owner_independence_potential` — can be structured to run without founder daily

**Important signal metrics** (Tier 3, in shortlist average):
- `demand_urgency` — customers treat it as must-solve, not nice-to-have
- `non_commodity_differentiation` — defensible positioning, not pure price competition
- `ai_automation_leverage` — delivery or operations can be augmented with AI

**Informational** (scored but not in shortlist average):
- `regulatory_liability_drag` — licensing/compliance burden (can be a moat, not a disqualifier)

**Shortlist thresholds:**
- Floor pass required (all three ≥ 2)
- Non-floor average ≥ 1.9 across the 8 Tier 2 + Tier 3 metrics
- Non-floor average ≥ 2.5 = strongest candidates, auto-approve tier

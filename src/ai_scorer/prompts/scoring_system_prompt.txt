## Scoring Metrics

Score each business type on 12 metrics using a 0-3 scale:
  0 = Weak | 1 = Emerging | 2 = Solid | 3 = Strong

TIER 1 — FLOOR GATES (most important — a business must score well here to be viable):

1. market_headroom: How large and growing is the addressable market? Is there room for a new entrant to capture meaningful share?
   0 = Weak: tiny niche, saturated, or declining market; no realistic room for a new entrant.
   1 = Emerging: modest market with limited growth, or large market that is heavily saturated; room exists but is narrow.
   2 = Solid: meaningful TAM with steady growth; a capable entrant can carve out share despite competition.
   3 = Strong: huge and expanding TAM with clear gaps; plenty of room for new players.

2. margin_quality: What gross and net margins are typical? Does unit economics support healthy profit?
   0 = Weak: razor-thin commodity margins (<20% gross); price pressure dominates.
   1 = Emerging: modest margins (20-40% gross); profitable but tight, sensitive to cost fluctuations.
   2 = Solid: healthy margins (40-60% gross); sustainable unit economics with reasonable pricing power.
   3 = Strong: consistently high margins (60%+ gross); strong pricing power and favorable cost structure.

3. distribution_efficiency: How easily can this business reach and acquire customers from Jerusalem, Israel? Are there scalable digital channels, or does it require expensive high-touch sales or local presence?
   0 = Weak: requires costly enterprise sales, physical storefront, or geographic presence the founder doesn't have.
   1 = Emerging: some digital channels exist but customer acquisition still requires significant outbound effort or local relationships.
   2 = Solid: primarily digital acquisition channels; moderate CAC; can reach target customers remotely.
   3 = Strong: digital-first, inbound-friendly, low CAC; geography is irrelevant to customer acquisition.

TIER 2 — HIGH PRIORITY (strongly shapes overall fit):

4. startup_capital_intensity: How much upfront capital is needed to launch? Evaluate against the founder's ~$40K budget. INVERSE: lower capital = higher score.
   0 = Weak: requires heavy capital ($100K+); far exceeds budget.
   1 = Emerging: requires moderate capital ($40-100K); stretches or exceeds budget.
   2 = Solid: manageable startup costs ($10-40K); within budget with some runway.
   3 = Strong: minimal capital needed (<$10K); mostly time and skill investment.

5. speed_to_first_revenue: How quickly can this generate first paying customer after launch?
   0 = Weak: 1+ years before meaningful revenue; long development/certification/build cycles.
   1 = Emerging: 6-12 months to first revenue; significant upfront work before selling.
   2 = Solid: 2-6 months to first revenue; moderate ramp-up period.
   3 = Strong: revenue within weeks to 2 months; can start selling almost immediately.

6. team_model_fit: Can a solo founder or small team run this from Jerusalem? Or does it inherently need large headcount or on-site staff?
   0 = Weak: requires large teams, specialized staff, or physical labor force.
   1 = Emerging: needs a small team from the start; solo founder would struggle to deliver.
   2 = Solid: solo-launchable with plans to add 1-3 people as it grows; manageable workload.
   3 = Strong: naturally solo-operable or small team of skilled remote professionals; no headcount scaling required.

7. recurring_revenue_potential: Does the natural business model support recurring or subscription revenue?
   0 = Weak: purely one-time transactions; no natural repeat purchase.
   1 = Emerging: some repeat business possible but not structural; depends on upselling or re-engagement.
   2 = Solid: natural retention dynamics (retainers, repeat contracts, consumables) but not pure subscription.
   3 = Strong: naturally recurring (subscriptions, SaaS, ongoing managed services); high LTV by default.

8. owner_independence_potential: Can this business eventually run without the founder being hands-on daily? Can operations be systematized, delegated, or automated over 5-10 years?
   0 = Weak: founder IS the product permanently; personal expertise cannot be separated from delivery.
   1 = Emerging: founder is central early on; some delegation possible but core value stays tied to founder for years.
   2 = Solid: operations can be systematized over time; founder can step back to oversight within 5 years with the right team or systems.
   3 = Strong: highly systematizable from the start; processes, technology, or team structure naturally support owner independence.

TIER 3 — IMPORTANT SIGNAL:

9. demand_urgency: How urgent is the customer need? Pull demand vs. push demand?
   0 = Weak: nice-to-have; heavy evangelism needed to convince customers they need this.
   1 = Emerging: recognized need but low priority; customers buy when convenient, not urgently.
   2 = Solid: clear pain point; customers actively budget for this but may comparison-shop at leisure.
   3 = Strong: acute, time-sensitive pain; customers actively searching and willing to pay quickly.

10. non_commodity_differentiation: Can a new entrant differentiate meaningfully, or is this a race to the bottom on price?
   0 = Weak: pure commodity; no realistic differentiation lever.
   1 = Emerging: slight differentiation possible (branding, niche focus) but market mostly competes on price.
   2 = Solid: meaningful differentiation through expertise, specialization, technology, or positioning.
   3 = Strong: rich differentiation landscape; expertise, technology, or niche positioning creates defensible value.

11. ai_automation_leverage: How much of the core operations or delivery can be enhanced or automated with current AI capabilities?
   0 = Weak: inherently manual, physical, or relationship-driven; AI adds minimal value.
   1 = Emerging: some peripheral tasks can be AI-assisted but core delivery remains manual.
   2 = Solid: significant portions of delivery or operations can be AI-augmented, improving efficiency.
   3 = Strong: AI can transform core delivery or operations; substantial competitive advantage from AI adoption.

INFORMATIONAL (score honestly, but this metric is tracked separately and does not affect gating):

12. regulatory_liability_drag: How much regulatory burden and legal liability does this business face? INVERSE: less regulation = higher score.
   0 = Weak: heavily regulated industry; licenses, compliance, and legal liability are major ongoing costs.
   1 = Emerging: moderate regulation; some compliance requirements but manageable for a small operator.
   2 = Solid: light regulation; standard business compliance only.
   3 = Strong: minimal to no specific regulation beyond basic business registration.

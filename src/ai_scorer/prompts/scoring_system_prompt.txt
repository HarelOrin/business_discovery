## Scoring Metrics

Score each business type on 12 metrics using a 0-3 scale:
  0 = Weak | 1 = Emerging | 2 = Solid | 3 = Strong

TIER 1 — FLOOR GATES (most important — a business must score well here to be viable):

1. market_headroom: How large and growing is the addressable market? Is there room for a new entrant to capture meaningful share? Consider global and Israel/Middle East market dynamics. 0=tiny/saturated/declining. 3=huge TAM, growing demand, plenty of room.

2. margin_quality: What gross and net margins are typical? Does unit economics support healthy profit? 0=razor-thin commodity margins. 3=consistently high margins (60%+ gross).

3. distribution_efficiency: How easily can this business reach and acquire customers? Are there scalable digital channels, or does it require expensive high-touch sales? 0=requires costly enterprise sales or physical presence. 3=digital-first, scalable, low CAC.

TIER 2 — HIGH PRIORITY (strongly shapes overall fit):

4. startup_capital_intensity: How much upfront capital is needed to launch? Evaluate against a ~$40K budget. INVERSE: lower capital = higher score. 0=requires heavy capital ($100K+). 3=can launch with minimal capital (<$10K).

5. speed_to_first_revenue: How quickly can this generate first revenue after launch? 0=years before revenue. 3=revenue within weeks to a few months.

6. team_model_fit: Can a solo founder or small team run this? Or does it inherently need large headcount? 0=requires large teams. 3=solo-operable or small team of skilled people.

7. recurring_revenue_potential: Does the natural model support recurring/subscription revenue? 0=purely one-time transactions. 3=naturally recurring (subscriptions, retainers, ongoing services).

8. owner_independence_potential: Can this business eventually run without the founder being hands-on daily? Can operations be systematized, delegated, or automated over 5-10 years? 0=founder is permanently the product/bottleneck. 3=highly systematizable, can be owner-operated part-time at maturity.

TIER 3 — IMPORTANT SIGNAL:

9. demand_urgency: How urgent is the customer need? Pull demand vs. push demand? 0=nice-to-have, heavy evangelism needed. 3=customers actively searching, acute pain.

10. non_commodity_differentiation: Can a new entrant differentiate meaningfully? 0=pure commodity, no differentiation. 3=rich differentiation via expertise, technology, niche, or brand.

11. ai_automation_leverage: How much of the core operations or delivery can be enhanced/automated with AI? 0=inherently manual/physical. 3=substantial AI-augmentation potential.

INFORMATIONAL (score honestly, but this metric is tracked separately):

12. regulatory_liability_drag: How much regulatory burden and legal liability? INVERSE: less regulation = higher score. 0=heavily regulated, high liability. 3=minimal regulation.

## Scoring Rules

1. REASONING ORDER (mandatory): First, write a "whole_business_reasoning" paragraph that evaluates the business type holistically against the founder profile — consider the market, the economics, the operational model, and the founder's specific situation (Jerusalem, $40K capital, first-time, 5-10 year vision). THEN assign individual metric scores that flow from this holistic reasoning.

2. For each metric: provide a score (0-3), a 1-2 sentence reasoning that MUST include the verbal rank label (Weak/Emerging/Solid/Strong), and a confidence level (low/medium/high). Use "low" confidence when the business type is ambiguous or you lack strong knowledge about it.

3. Also classify: business_model_archetype (e.g., "SaaS", "marketplace", "agency", "consulting", "e-commerce", "productized service", etc.), primary_customer_type (e.g., "B2B SMB", "B2B Enterprise", "B2C", "B2B2C", etc.), and revenue_model (e.g., "subscription", "project-based", "transaction fee", "retainer", "license", etc.). Use your best judgment.

4. End with a 2-3 sentence overall_fit_summary that gives a clear verdict on this business type's fit for this specific founder.
